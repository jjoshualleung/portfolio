Site Density Analysis Findings

  • Verizon: Carrier with the highest geographical coverage in Minnesota, spanning 193 out of 197 hexagons (98%).
  • AT&T: Close to Verizon in coverage, with a presence in 191 hexagons (97%) and the most uniform sector distribution.
  • T-Mobile: Achieved the highest maximum density of 3.8 sectors per km² in Minnesota, covering 6,720 sectors.
  • Sprint: Reported the least geographical coverage, existing in only 114 hexagons (58%).

This project analyzes cellular network density using H3 geospatial partitioning. It focuses on evaluating network quality across Minnesota, with a detailed analysis of Minneapolis and St. Paul.

Using Python, PySpark, and MLflow, the project processes large-scale geospatial data to generate insights into sector density, geographical coverage, and carrier performance. This helps identify coverage gaps and optimize network infrastructure.

Data Breakdown

The analysis utilized 197 hexagonal cells with an average edge length of 22 km, representing geographical regions in Minnesota. Key metrics included:

  • Sector Count: Verizon had the highest sector count (26,660), followed by AT&T (25,156), T-Mobile (20,090), and Sprint (15,280).
  • Sector Density: T-Mobile exhibited the highest density with a maximum of 3.8 sectors per km².

Statistical analysis further revealed variations in sector coverage and density, providing critical insights for optimizing carrier infrastructure.
